Beyond the Lake

By Jim Urquhart

As we sail through the silent forest on our sleds, large flakes fall slowly on the Sinks of Northern Utah. The Sinks are a natural limestone sinkhole in Logan Canyon, and have the dubious distinction of being the coldest spot in Utah. They are also known for the second-coldest recorded temperature in the lower 48 states, -69.3 degrees Fahrenheit. Single file, we follow a lonely path through a massive field of white. Off the trail the powder is deep, billowy, and inviting. In the distance the trees provide contrast — this is where our options expand.

Thankfully, it's not that cold when I visit, though I've layered appropriately and prepared for the experience. And while those extreme, cold temperatures can translate to light snow, I am told, there are plenty of beautiful, sunny, blue-sky days at the Sinks. Today is overcast, however, and seemingly solitary — a little unexpected for a Saturday afternoon.
